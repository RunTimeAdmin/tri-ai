### Key Observation
**There is NO polished, consumer-facing, web-based product that lets users submit any topic and watch three distinct AI agents debate through a structured dialectical process with ranked consensus output.** The space is dominated by:
1. Academic papers (theory, not products)
2. Developer frameworks (AutoGen, LangGraph — requires coding)
3. Simple scripts (two models arguing back and forth)
4. Enterprise tools (not accessible to regular users)

## 🎯 DIFFERENTIATION STRATEGY

### What Makes Ours UNIQUE vs. Everything Else:

**1. Consumer-First, Not Developer-First**
- AutoGen, LangGraph, n8n = require coding
- Ours = type a question, watch the debate unfold

**2. Structured Dialectical Process (4 Phases)**
- Most tools: agents just argue back and forth randomly
- Ours: Opening → Cross-Examination → Rebuttal → Synthesis
- This mirrors real academic/legal debate structure

**3. Named Agents with Distinct Personalities**
- Most tools: "Agent 1, Agent 2" or "Model A, Model B"
- Ours: CIPHER (Skeptic), NOVA (Advocate), PRISM (Synthesizer)
- Each has a defined reasoning approach, not just a different model

**4. Ranked Consensus Output**
- Most tools: just show the conversation and stop
- Ours: produces specific, ranked conclusions (#1, #2, #3) with confidence levels
- Plus remaining disagreements — intellectual honesty

**5. Real Research Integration**
- Most debate tools: agents argue from training data only
- Ours: agents research the web first, then debate with real data and citations

**6. Transparency of Process**
- Show the full reasoning chain, not just the answer
- Users can see WHERE the agents disagreed and WHY they converged
- Educational value — teaches critical thinking

**7. Domain Awareness**
- Crypto/Web3 domain expertise (David's specialty)
- Agents understand tokenomics, DeFi, blockchain architecture
- Not just generic "AI debates AI" — domain-informed analysis
