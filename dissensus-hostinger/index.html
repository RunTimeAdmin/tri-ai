The Dissensus Process

Four phases. Zero bias.
One truth.

Dissensus follows a structured dialectical process modeled after academic debate and adversarial testing. Every topic goes through four rigorous phases.

1

📢 Opening Statements

Each agent independently researches the topic and presents their initial perspective. Three distinct viewpoints, backed by real data.

2

⚔️ Cross-Examination

The agents challenge each other's reasoning. They point out flaws, biases, missing data, and logical fallacies in each other's arguments.

3

🛡️ Rebuttals & Defense

Each agent refines their position based on the challenges received. Weak arguments are abandoned. Strong ones are reinforced with additional evidence.

4

🎯 Synthesis & Consensus

The agents converge on ranked conclusions with confidence levels. Remaining disagreements are transparently documented. Truth, forged through conflict.
